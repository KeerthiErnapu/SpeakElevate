Tenses

Tenses are used to indicate the time of action. There are three main tenses in English: Present, Past, and Future. Each tense has four forms:

1. Present Tense:

Indicates actions happening right now or regularly. Example: I play football every day.

2. Past Tense:

Describes actions that have already happened. Example: I played football yesterday.

3. Future Tense:

Indicates actions that will happen. Example: I will play football tomorrow.

4. Present Continuous:

Describes actions happening now. Example: I am playing football right now.

5. Past Continuous:

Describes actions that were happening at a specific point in the past. Example: I was playing football when you called me.

6. Future Continuous:

Describes actions that will be happening at a specific point in the future. Example: I will be playing football at 5 PM tomorrow.

7. Present Perfect:

Describes actions that have happened at an unspecified time. Example: I have played football before.

8. Past Perfect:

Describes actions that were completed before another event in the past. Example: I had played football before it started raining.

9. Future Perfect:

Describes actions that will be completed by a certain time in the future. Example: I will have played football by 6 PM.

Articles

Articles are used to define nouns. There are two types of articles in English:

1. Definite Article:

The is used to refer to something specific. Example: The book on the table is mine.

2. Indefinite Articles:

A and An are used to refer to non-specific things. Example: A dog is barking outside. An apple a day keeps the doctor away.
